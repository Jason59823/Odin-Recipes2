Mexican (Morris Nachos)

Description

This is a recipe for an absolute staple in my household, we often referred to this recipe as "mexican" however they are just a standard nachos dish with a much simpler approach, however, still an absolute favourite to me and my family.

Ingredients

  • Tortilla chips
  • Mince
  • Taco Seasoning
  • Shredded Lettuce
  • 1 Tomato
  • Sour Cream
  • Grated Cheese

Steps

  1. Step 1: Begin by heating up a frying pan and covering the surface of the pan with some cooking oil (any kind will work for this recipe)
  2. Step 2: Place the entire packet of mince into the frying pan and cook on medium heat until you no longer see any pink in the mince
  3. Step 3: While the mince is cooking, begin preparing your lettuce and tomato as this is the only time consuming step in the recipe that you need to do whilst the mince is cooking. If your lettuce has not come pre shredded then shred away to whatever size you and your company prefer, same as the tomatos, dice them as small as you want however you generally dont want the peices smaller then 1cm
  4. Step 4: Once the mince is no longer pink, begin adding your chosen spices to the frying pan with a small amount of water so it doesnt completely dry the mince out
  5. Step 5: Split your tortilla chips to the serving plates as this will serve as the base of the dish
  6. Step 6: Add the mince directly on top of the tortilla chips and begin adding the other ingredients on top (the traditional Morris way is to add the lettuce and tomato first, then a dollop of the sour cream and then finally add your grated cheese)
